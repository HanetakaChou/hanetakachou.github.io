Radiosity

Radiant Existance

The term radiosity B is a deprecated term which was popular in the last century, and currently should be called radiant exitance M instead which denotes the outgoing irradiance E.

The terms irradiance E and form factor F may be used interchangeably, but technically irradiance E should NOT be divided by π \displaystyle \pi π. This means that E ⁡ = π F ⁡ \displaystyle \operatorname{E} = \pi \operatorname{F} E=πF.

However, to be consistent with the most literature on the Radiosity global illumination algorithm, the term radiosity B rather than radiant exitance M is used here.
